Re: Just destroyed my emonTx Shield while soldering the jumpers :(

Submitted by Robert Wall on Wed, 25/09/2013 - 15:08.

The first question is, how did you manage to destroy the p.c.b?  Are you using the correct size soldering iron and is the temperature correct? (It is as bad not hot enough as too hot!) You probably took too long to make the joint, you should be able to complete that link in less than one second.  It's too late now, but with fine work like this, it is essential to get these things right.

If you look at / download the design files for the Shield from Solderpad (there's a link on the Shield page), you'll see the pads go to header pins and a 4k7 resistor R18. You might be able to solder a fine wire link (insulated of course) on the underside of the board from the resistor to the appropriate header pin.

Re: Just destroyed my emonTx Shield while soldering the jumpers :(

Submitted by calypso_rae on Wed, 25/09/2013 - 15:23.

The layout of the emonTx Shield is available on Solderpad in the Reference section.  With fine wire, such as "Kynar", you should be able to rectify any problems.  If a track has been destroyed, you can just tack the wire directly to the pad of the RFM12B chip. 

It may be helpful to route the wire through a spare hole so that it can be terminated at the correct place on the "solder" side of the board.   Most errors can be fixed with a bit of care.

Good Luck!
