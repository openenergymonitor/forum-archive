Re: DG Synchronisation For Solar Inverter

Submitted by Robert Wall on Sun, 13/09/2015 - 13:03.

I do not understand what you are trying to achieve. You are assuming that anyone reading your post knows a lot more about your system than you have told us.

Are you talking about using the diesel generator whilst connected to the grid? Why should you want to do that?

Or are you talking about using the inverter and the diesel generator to support a local load whilst off-grid?

Your immediate problem is the grid-tie inverter is designed to work only when it has a stable grid connection, and it is designed to shut down when that grid connection disappears. This is so that it does not feed into the grid when your part of the grid has been isolated so that linesmen can work on the system in safety. That is also the reason why you must completely isolate yourself from the grid before connecting the diesel generator to your load.

So as well as controlling the outputs of both the diesel generator and the inverter, you also need to convince the inverter that it is safe to operate.

If you want to use both the diesel and the inverter together off-grid, I can envisage massive problems and you need to get the manufacturer of the inverter and the manufacturer of the diesel generator talking to one another.

I do not know of anyone here with the specialist detailed knowledge of both pieces of equipment who can advise you.
