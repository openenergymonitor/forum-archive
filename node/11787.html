Re: Plug&Play Solar & Three phase household

Submitted by Robert Wall on Fri, 11/12/2015 - 13:37.

The emonTx can, with a voltage input from an ac adapter, detect the direction of power flow.

You would need an emonTx, ac adapter & 3 CTs. You would also need a programmer as the pre-loaded sketch is for single phase only (as is usual in domestic installations in the UK), and you will need to load the 3-phase sketch.
(I understand that a 3-phase emonTx is under consideration, but if it happens, it won't happen for a while.)

If it is possible to run a cable ('microphone'-type two-core overall screened) from your balcony to the emonTx, a 4th CT will give you solar production.

Then you need a way to see the results. An emonGLCD (when available from the shop) will give you the present values, or you can add a Raspberry Pi with radio to send the data to emoncms.org and/or function as a local web server to give you web pages on your LAN.
(An emonPi won't help you as it has only 2 inputs for current.)

I don't know whether your smart plug would be capable of integrating with emoncms, or whether it would necessarily remain separate.

You could also look at the 3-phase Mk2 PV Router.
