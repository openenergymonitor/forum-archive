Submitted by mharizanov on Thu, 15/01/2015 - 05:40.

Don't use 5V on the RFM2Pi because that will kill the radio module, it is a strictly 3.3V device;
Powering the Raspberry Pi from GPIOs may be risky, but possible: see http://www.raspberrypi.org/forums/viewtopic.php?f=44&t=10500

Submitted by Eric_AMANN on Fri, 16/01/2015 - 15:08.

Hi,

I met a similar problem when trying to connect a LCD screen to my Pi (equipped with a RFM12Pi).

I used the P5 header which is a secondary GPIO port. You will have to do some soldering. In my case, I soldered a right-angled male header on top of the board. Sorry, no photo, but everything goes in a standard case.

Eric

EDIT : the P5 header is only available on the Model B Rev2
