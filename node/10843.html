Re: Optical Utility Meter LED Pulse Sensor an EmonTH

Submitted by pb66 on Wed, 17/06/2015 - 10:35.

Hi Brian - You can add a mini usb socket to the emonTH if you remove the battery carrier ( and make a hole in the case) as the pcb has the footprint there it just isn't fitted. Or you could power via the ftdi header or the same terminal block a sensor could connect to.

So while the "new Optical Utility Meter LED Pulse Sensor" will not connect directly to the emonTH as it has no RJ45 socket and the batteries aren't up to it, you can count pulses with a powered emonTH and some minimal modding to interconnect the sensor, power supply and emonTH.

​Paul

Re: Optical Utility Meter LED Pulse Sensor an EmonTH

Submitted by pb66 on Tue, 23/06/2015 - 22:08.

No need to chop the sensor, just source a RJ45 socket, a socket to match your power adapter and interlink them with the tails entering a vent on the emonTH into the screw terminals.

To make a neater more permanent solution, remove the emonTH battery carrier, fit the mini usb and glue a RJ45 socket on it's side, to the pcb where the non-terminal end of the battery carrier was. with a couple of well place holes for the RJ45 and  power plug it would be a proper mod.
