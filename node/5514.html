Firstly, there was no need for a title to your post that uses language that might be taken by some to be offensive.

If you search this site, you will find very many reports of a similar problem, with roughly the same explanation each time. You may well be misunderstanding something, but you have given no details of the current transformer that you are using, nor any other significant details - not even what the column of numbers refers to, so it is hard to judge whether the readings you see are reasonable for an Arduino board, or not. If I assume you have not changed the sketch and you have a 100 A c.t, then you are seeing readings that represent about 0.4% of the maximum reading of apparent power.

Re: Help - EmonTxShield V3 - measuring unexplained values?

Submitted by Robert Wall on Tue, 05/08/2014 - 19:59.

Have you tried searching? Try reading Measurement implications of ADC resolution at low current values, http://openenergymonitor.org/emon/node/4822, http://openenergymonitor.org/emon/node/5303.

What you are reading is almost certainly noise from the digital part of the microprocessor being picked up by the analogue to digital converter and being measured. The emonTx V3 was carefully designed to include as much filtering and shielding as possible to reduce this. As far as I know, the Arduino boards have very little filtering and shielding, if any.
